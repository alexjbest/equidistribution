Problem

Distribution of

\begin{equation*} \mathscr H = \{ (x,y,z) \in \ZZ^3 : x^2 + y^2 + z^2 = d\} \end{equation*}

as \(d \to \infty\text{.}\)

Theorem 3.1 Linnik

As \(d \to \infty\) squarefree \(d \equiv \pm 1 \pmod 5\)

\begin{equation*} \left\{ \frac {x}{\sqrt d} : x \in \mathscr H \right\} \end{equation*}

is equidistributed w.r.t. the Lebesgue measure

\begin{equation*} \red_\infty \colon \mathscr H_d \to S^2 \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \frac{|\red_\infty\inv (\Omega)|}{|\mathscr H_d|} \to \operatorname{Vol} (\Omega) \end{equation*}

\(\Omega\) nice.

A variant

Theorem 3.2 Linnik variant
\begin{equation*} \mathscr H_d( q) = \{ x^2 + y^2 + z^3 = d \pmod q \} \end{equation*}

with \(q\) fixed \(\gt 0\) and \((q,30) =1\text{.}\) As \(d \to \infty\text{,}\) \(d\) squarefree and \(d \equiv \pm 1 \pmod 5\text{.}\) \(\overline {\mathscr H}_d\) becomes equidistributed in \(\mathscr H_d(q)\) w.r.t measure.

\begin{equation*} \frac{| \red\inv_q(x)|}{| \mathscr H_d|} = \frac{1}{|\mathscr H_d(q)|} ( 1+ o(1)) \end{equation*}

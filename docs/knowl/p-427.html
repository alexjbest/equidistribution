Let \(\Sigma(d,l,q)\) denote

\begin{equation*} \#\{(x,x') \in \mathscr H_d\times \mathscr H_d : \gamma_x^{(l)} = \gamma_{x'}^{(l)} \text{ in } \mathscr H_d\} \end{equation*}

where

\begin{equation*} \gamma_x^{(l)}\in \mathscr H_d(q) \end{equation*}

is the chain cut at \(\lb -l,\rb\) and then reduced mod \(q\text{.}\) Then

\begin{equation*} \sigma (l,d,q) \ll_\epsilon |\mathscr H_d| + d^\epsilon (1+ \frac{d}{q^2 5^{2l}}) \end{equation*}

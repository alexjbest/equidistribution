Theorem 3.48

Let \(g \in L^2(\Gamma \backslash X)\text{.}\) Then

\begin{equation*} g(n) = \pair{g}{u_1} u_1(n) + \pair{g}{u_2} u_2(n) + 2 \pi \int_0^\pi \pair{g }{\tilde f _\theta} \tilde f _\theta \frac{\diff \theta}{(q-1)^2 - 4q \sin^2 \theta} \end{equation*}

where

\begin{equation*} u_1 = \sqrt{\frac{q^2-1}{2q}},\, u_2 = \sqrt{\frac{q^2-1}{2q}} (-1)^n\text{.} \end{equation*}
